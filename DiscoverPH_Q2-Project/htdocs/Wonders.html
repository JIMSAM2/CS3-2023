EXPLORE PARADISE:

THE BEST OF THE PHILLIPPINES

Wonders

Embark on an awe-inspiring journey across the Philippines, where the wonders of Luzon, Visayas, and Mindanao weave a captivating tapestry of natural marvels. In Luzon, the ancient Banaue Rice Terraces and the iconic Mayon Volcano stand testament to the ingenuity and majesty of nature. Transitioning to the central Visayas, Bohol's Chocolate Hills and the underwater paradise of Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park beckon with their extraordinary beauty. Explore the serene Bantayan Island or the vibrant nightlife of Boracay. Venture south to Mindanao, where the Enchanted River in Hinatuan and the towering Mount Apo offer mesmerizing landscapes. Island-hop through the Britania Islands, discover the cultural richness of Lake Sebu, and witness the majestic Aliwagwag Falls in Davao Oriental. Join us on this whirlwind odyssey as we unveil the diverse wonders that define the Philippines, each a testament to the country's rich natural heritage.

LET'S GO!

LUZON

Banaue Rice Terraces

Carved into the mountains of Ifugao, the Banaue Rice Terraces are a marvel of ancient engineering, representing the ingenuity of the Ifugao people who shaped the landscape for centuries with their hands.

Mayon Volcano

With its perfect cone, Mayon Volcano in Albay is both majestic and volatile. It stands as an iconic symbol of nature's beauty and power, captivating visitors with its breathtaking views.

Taal Volcano

Situated in Batangas, Taal Volcano is a geological wonder within a lake, housing a crater within a crater. The surrounding Taal Lake offers a surreal setting for exploration and appreciation of nature's geological artistry.

Pagsanjan Falls

Found in Laguna, Pagsanjan Falls enchants with its cascading waters set against a lush backdrop. Adventurous boat rides through the river gorge provide an exhilarating experience for nature enthusiasts.

Baguio City

Known as the "Summer Capital of the Philippines," Baguio City offers cool mountain air, vibrant markets, and a rich arts scene. The city's picturesque landscapes and cultural charm make it a favorite destination for relaxation and creativity.

Intramuros

Intramuros, meaning "within the walls," is a historic district in Manila showcasing colonial-era architecture and landmarks. Steeped in Spanish colonial history, it offers a glimpse into the Philippines' past.

VISAYAS

Chocolate Hills

Bohol's Chocolate Hills form a unique landscape of conical hills that turn brown during the dry season, resembling chocolate mounds. The geological wonder draws visitors seeking the extraordinary in nature.

Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park

Located in the Sulu Sea, Tubbataha Reefs Natural Park is a UNESCO World Heritage Site known for its pristine coral reefs. Divers flock to its underwater paradise to explore vibrant marine ecosystems.

Apo Island

Off the coast of Negros Oriental, Apo Island is a marine sanctuary teeming with diverse marine life. Snorkelers and divers are captivated by the colorful coral gardens and encounters with sea turtles.

Sohoton Cave

Nestled in Basey, Samar, Sohoton Cave entices adventurers with its captivating limestone formations and an underground river. The cave system offers an otherworldly experience for those exploring its hidden wonders.

Boracay Island

Boracay, known for its powdery white sand beaches and vibrant nightlife, remains a premier tropical destination. The island's stunning sunsets and water activities attract sun-seekers from around the world.

Bantayan Island

Bantayan Island in Cebu boasts pristine white sand beaches and crystal-clear waters. Its laid-back atmosphere and natural beauty make it an ideal escape for those seeking tranquility.

MINDANAO

Enchanted River

Hinatuan's Enchanted River is a mesmerizing blue lagoon surrounded by lush landscapes. Its mysterious allure and vibrant underwater life make it a must-visit destination for nature enthusiasts.

Mount Apo

As the highest peak in the Philippines, Mount Apo is a majestic stratovolcano offering panoramic views and diverse ecosystems. Climbers are rewarded with a sense of accomplishment and breathtaking landscapes.

Lake Holon

Tucked in the mountains of South Cotabato, Lake Holon is a serene crater lake surrounded by lush forests. Camping by the lake provides an immersive experience with nature.

Britania Islands

The Britania Islands, located in Surigao del Sur, boast pristine beaches and turquoise waters. Island-hopping through this picturesque archipelago reveals hidden coves and untouched beauty.

Lake Sebu

Lake Sebu is a captivating landscape in South Cotabato, known for its three mountain lakes and the vibrant culture of the T'boli people. Visitors can explore the serene lakes and learn about the indigenous heritage.

Aliwagwag Falls

Aliwagwag Falls in Cateel, Davao Oriental, stands as one of the highest waterfalls in the Philippines. Its series of cascading tiers surrounded by lush greenery creates a mesmerizing natural spectacle.
