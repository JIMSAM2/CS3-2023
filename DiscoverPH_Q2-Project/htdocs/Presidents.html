Presidents of the Philippines

Emilio Aguinaldo 1899 - 1901

March 22, 1869 - February 6, 1964

Aguinaldo became the first President of the Philippines during the country's struggle for independence from Spanish rule. However, his presidency faced challenges, including the Philippine-American War.

Read more
Manuel L. Quezon 1935 - 1944

August 19, 1878 - August 1, 1944

Quezon served as the first President of the Commonwealth of the Philippines. His term was marked by efforts to strengthen the country's economy and preparations for its eventual independence.

Read more
Jose P. Laurel 1943 - 1945

March 9, 1891 - November 6, 1959

Laurel assumed the presidency during the Japanese occupation. His controversial collaboration with the Japanese has been a subject of historical scrutiny.

Read more
Sergio Osmeña 1944 - 1946

September 9, 1878 - October 19, 1961

Osmeña succeeded Quezon and led the country towards recovery after World War II, addressing the challenges of reconstruction and rehabilitation.

Read more
Manuel Roxas 1946 - 1948

January 1, 1892 - April 15, 1948

Roxas became the first president of the independent Republic of the Philippines. His presidency focused on post-war recovery and economic development.

Read more
Elpidio Quirino 1948 - 1953

November 16, 1890 - February 29, 1956

Quirino's term was marked by efforts to stabilize the economy and address post-war challenges, but it also faced issues of corruption and political unrest.

Read more
Ramon Magsaysay 1953 - 1957

August 31, 1907 - March 17, 1957

Magsaysay was known for his anti-corruption initiatives and efforts to improve the lives of ordinary Filipinos. His presidency was tragically cut short by a plane crash.

Read more
Carlos P. Garcia 1957 - 1961

November 4, 1896 - June 14, 1971

Garcia's term focused on Filipino First Policy, advocating for economic independence. His presidency saw developments in trade and agriculture.

Read more
Diosdado Macapagal 1961 - 1965

September 28, 1910 - April 21, 1997

Macapagal pursued economic reforms and land redistribution during his term. He is also known for moving Independence Day to June 12 to honor the declaration of Philippine independence.

Read more
Ferdinand Marcos 1965 - 1986

September 11, 1917 - September 28, 1989

Marcos declared martial law in 1972, extending his rule beyond constitutional limits. While his regime initially saw economic growth, it was marred by human rights abuses, corruption, and eventually led to the People Power Revolution, forcing him into exile.

Read more
Corazon Aquino 1986 - 1992

January 25, 1933 - August 1, 2009

Aquino, the first female president in Asia, led the Philippines through a period of political transition after the People Power Revolution. Her presidency focused on restoring democracy and instituting reforms.

Read more
Fidel V. Ramos 1992 - 1998

March 18, 1928 - July 31, 2022

Ramos' presidency is noted for economic reforms and infrastructure development. He played a crucial role in stabilizing the economy and improving international relations.

Read more
Joseph Estrada 1998 - 2001

Born April 19, 1937

Estrada's term faced economic challenges, and his presidency was cut short by a peaceful uprising, EDSA II, due to allegations of corruption.

Read more
Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo 2001 - 2010

Born April 5, 1947

Arroyo's presidency saw economic growth, but it was marred by controversies, including allegations of corruption. She served the remainder of Estrada's term and was elected for a full term.

Read more
Benigno Aquino III 2010 - 2016

February 8, 1960 - June 24, 2021

Aquino focused on anti-corruption initiatives and economic reforms during his term. His presidency was marked by improved economic performance and international recognition.

Read more
Rodrigo Duterte 2016 - 2022

Born March 28, 1945

Duterte's presidency is marked by a controversial war on drugs, a shift in foreign policy, and efforts to address infrastructure development. However, it has also faced criticism for human rights concerns and authoritarian tendencies.

Read more
Bongbong Marcos 2022 - Present

Born September 13, 1957

Ferdinand Romualdez Marcos Jr., commonly known as Bongbong, was inaugurated as the 17th President of the Philippines on May 9, 2022, and has dedicated his life to serving the Filipino people for over two decades. He has vowed to be a leader for every Filipino and is committed to elevating the quality of life for all.
